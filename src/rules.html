Checkers

  • The darker color pieces always move first.
  • Pieces can only move diagonally.
  • A piece can eat another if it can jump over it.
  • To jump over a piece there has to be an opponent's piece next to it, followed by a free space.
  • Multiple jumps in one play are allowed as long as they are consecutive.
  • Men can only move forward.
  • When jumping, the first jump has to be forward, any other consecutive jump can be in any direction.
  • When a man reaches the other side of the board it has to stay there for that play.
  • When a man reaches the other side of the board it becomes a king.
  • Kings can move both forward and backward.
  • Kings can jump in any direction.
  • If one of your pieces can jump, they have to jump.
  • You win when a player has no more pieces or when they can't move their pieces.
  • Players draw when both can't move their pieces.
